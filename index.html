About Me

I'm an internationally published, award winning photographer and multi-instrumentalist residing in Richmond, VA. I have a Bachelor of Arts in Music (Specification in Audio Engineering and Digital Recording) and a Minor in Creative Advertising from Virginia Commonwealth University. In 2006, I founded Fire At Will Photography. Since its inception, I have been published in the US, UK and Russia by Rolling Stone, MSN, Buzzfeed and more. In 2015, I received the Mark of Excellence Award by the Society of Professional Journalists at the University of Maryland for best supporting photo in a publication. In 2013, I cofounded Greyskull Studios, a music recording studio based in Colonial Heights, Virginia.


From 2015-2016, I served as the Primary Educator at Longwood University's Center for the Visual Arts, where I expanded the ArtKids program, a special outreach program that teaches photography to gifted and at-risk fourth graders at Cumberland County Elementary School and Prince Edward County Elementary School. In 2016, I founded The Cold Collective, a multimedia group and artist community dedicated to bridging the gap between the fragmented and compartmentalized art and music scenes of central Virginia.

​

I'm changing gears to a career in coding because the future isn't going to build and program itself. I am currently a student at University of Richmond's Coding Bootcamp and teach music lessons on oboe, saxophone and piano.
